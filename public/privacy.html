Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 26, 2026

Short version: We collect the minimum data needed to run the service. We never sell your data, show you ads, or track you across the web. Family stories stay private by design.

1. Who we are

Bloodline is a family history application operated by an individual based in Canada. "We," "us," and "our" refer to the operator of Bloodline at myfamilybloodline.com.

We are subject to Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial privacy legislation in Ontario.

2. What we collect

We collect only what is necessary to provide the service:

  • Email address — for passwordless authentication (magic link / 6-digit code). We do not store passwords.
  • Family tree data — names, dates, relationships, biographies, photos, and memories that you enter. This data belongs to you.
  • Usage metadata — last sign-in timestamp, which features you use. No fine-grained behavioural tracking.
  • Device information — browser type and operating system version, collected automatically for debugging. Not linked to individual profiles.

We do not collect payment information, social media profiles, location data, or any data from third parties.

3. How we use your information

  • Authenticate you via magic link (email + one-time code)
  • Store and synchronise your family tree across devices
  • Send transactional emails: sign-in codes and family invitations you initiate
  • Diagnose errors and improve the service

We never use your data for advertising, profiling, or sale to third parties.

4. Privacy for living people and children

Bloodline is designed with family privacy as a first principle:

  • Living people are marked as private by default. Only family members you invite can see their full profile.
  • Children (minors) receive additional protection — their records are never exposed publicly and are not indexed by search engines.
  • Health conditions and sensitive family history stored in Bloodline are encrypted at rest and visible only to family members with appropriate access.

5. Data storage and transfers

Your family tree data is stored in the European Union (Cloudflare R2, EU jurisdiction) and globally distributed edge databases (Cloudflare D1) for performance. By using Bloodline, you consent to this storage and processing.

We use Cloudflare Pages, Workers, D1, and R2 for infrastructure, and Brevo for transactional email. These providers are bound by data processing agreements consistent with PIPEDA requirements.

6. Data retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, your personal information and family tree data are deleted within 30 days, except where retention is required by law.

Photos and documents uploaded to Bloodline are stored in our cloud storage and are deleted when you remove them or delete your account.

7. Your rights under PIPEDA

Under Canadian privacy law, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Withdraw consent (which may limit service functionality)
  • Request deletion of your account and associated data
  • Know how your data is used

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at the email address in section 10.

8. Cookies and local storage

We use one session cookie (httpOnly, secure, SameSite=Lax) to maintain your authenticated session. We use browser localStorage to cache your family tree for offline access and performance. We do not use advertising cookies, third-party tracking pixels, or analytics cookies.

9. Future monetisation

Bloodline is currently provided free of charge. We reserve the right to introduce paid features or subscription plans in the future. If we do, any paid tier will be clearly disclosed, and free tier functionality will be communicated in advance. We will not retroactively monetise data you have already provided, and we will never sell your personal information or family data regardless of business model changes.

10. Contact

For privacy questions, data requests, or to report a concern, please contact the operator directly through the app's feedback channel or the email address associated with your account.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the service evolves. Material changes will be communicated by email to registered users at least 14 days before taking effect. The "last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision.

12. Governing law

This Privacy Policy is governed by the laws of the Province of Ontario and the federal laws of Canada applicable therein. Any disputes shall be resolved in the courts of Ontario.
